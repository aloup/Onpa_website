Description

We are creating a database of music artists.

Whereas most music databases focus on the music output, we would like to create a comprehensive database that encompasses all artists involved in music production. “All artists” means not just the performers themselves, but producers, songwriters, choreographers, video directors, and so on. We will also track artistic output: songs, videos, choreography, and so on, as well as more personal information such as awards and biographical information about the artists.

The goal of the database is to provide information to people who are interested in all aspects of music production.

Conclusion

In this project, our group will create a database of music artists. Users should be able to search artists by role (e.g. performer, producer, choreographer), to search songs to see which artists worked on them, and to see links to examples of artists’ work (such as music videos or choreography).

The ER diagram shows the primary relationships between our different entities: the artists and the work they produce. There are also many attributes for each entity, fitting with the descriptive nature of this database as an information source. The primary relationships are:

  • ARTISTS write SONGS which accompany VIDEOS.
  • SONGS comprise ALBUMS.
  • SONGS are part of set lists on TOURS.
  • ARTISTS create VIDEOS which contain CHOREOGRAPHY.
  • ARTISTS go on TOURS.

During the process of creating this database, our group has faced a few problems. Since the platform is complex, we find it difficult to compress and include all the essential components without making the database overcrowded. It has also been challenging to describe all the complexities with the limitations of an ER diagram; the EER diagram we have created should serve this database much better.

It is also difficult to nail down the degree of granularity we will be going for: for example, how many possible roles are there in music production? Is a songwriter different from a lyricist, who is different from a composer? We will have to establish clear definitions of the terms we use and how they interrelate. This also means having descriptive, clear names for all entities and attributes, to minimize confusion.
